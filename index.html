Childhood

1919 - The Great War was over, the Titanic had sunk, and the Warner Brothers had opened their first film studio. Ronald Bevis Spencer was born into a time of change, almost 1,000,000 British citizens had lost their lives in the war and the country was slowly rebuilding.

Son of Rose Spencer, Ronald never knew his father and was kicked out of home at a young age. He lived with a friend’s family, before enlisting for the army when the Second World War broke out.

War

1939 - The Second World War broke out when Ronald was just 20 years old. After not having a solid family growing up he said that the army was the closest thing to a family that he had.

Ron spent three years in Africa as a Desert Rat in the artillery division. During this period he fought in the Second Battle of El Alamein, as well as in the Doomsday Battle.

Immigration

1949 - Ron left South Hampton for Australia under the Australian Government's Ten Pound Pom scheme. A passenger on the Largs Bay ship, it took him five weeks to complete the journey, arriving in Fremantle in October.

Work

1949 - When he first arrived in Australia, Ron was placed at the Snowy Mountain scheme, building the hydroelectricity and irrigation complex. He stayed here for a few years, before moving back to Melbourne in 1953.

Family

1955 - Whilst in hospital recovering from an eye injury, Ron met Joan Elizabeth Leigh, a 26 year old nursing sister from Oakleigh. He was ten years older than her and working as an Instrument maker. A year later they married.

1962: Ron and Joan welcomed their only child, Ann Marie at Mercy hospital, Brighton.

1993: Ron had two grandchildren, his eldest Alice Claire Smith was born in 1993, and his youngest (and favourite) was born three years later.

Death

1999 - At the age of 80 Ronald Bevis Spencer died of Bowel Cancer, at Knox Private Hospital in Melbourne. His wife and family were by his side.
